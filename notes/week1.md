## Day 1 — Conceptual Foundations (Section 1.4 reading)

### The Three Core Concepts

- **Arm**: A single action choice available to the agent — in our context, one inspection
  strategy (e.g. "full structural", "fire safety only"). The agent pulls one arm per step
  and cannot observe what the other arms would have returned.

- **Reward**: The numerical signal received after pulling an arm — in our context, the
  loss-prevention value generated by an inspection (higher = better risk reduction per £
  spent). Rewards are stochastic: the same inspection strategy returns slightly different
  value each time due to property variation, surveyor judgment, and claim volatility.

- **Regret**: The cumulative opportunity cost of not always pulling the best arm. At each
  step, regret = μ* − μ(aₜ), where μ* is the true best arm mean and μ(aₜ) is the mean
  of the chosen arm. Total regret after T steps measures how much value was left on the
  table during learning. A good algorithm minimises regret quickly.

---

### Why InspectionBanditEnv is a genuine bandit problem

Each inspection decision is **independent** — the reward from today's fire safety
inspection does not change the reward distribution of tomorrow's structural inspection.
There is no state that evolves. This is the defining criterion of a bandit vs an MDP.

## Day 1 — ε-Greedy Class Structure (one-liners)

- `__init__`: stores n_arms, epsilon (exploration rate), epsilon_decay, epsilon_min, and
  initialises Q-value estimates `q` and pull counts `n` as zero arrays, plus step counter `t`.

- `select()`: with probability ε draws a **random** arm (explore), otherwise returns
  `argmax(q)` — the arm with the highest current estimated value (exploit).

- `update(arm, reward)`: increments `n[arm]`, applies **incremental mean update**
  `q[arm] += (reward − q[arm]) / n[arm]`, then decays epsilon by multiplying by
  epsilon_decay (floored at epsilon_min).

## Day 1 — Domain Insight

> ε = 0.01 → the underwriter who always uses the same inspection method.  
> ε = 0.30 → the team that never commits to a strategy.  
> ε decay → how a mature insurer should actually behave: explore early, exploit late.

The InspectionBanditEnv **true best arm is Arm 0** (full structural, mean=0.70).  
ε-greedy with decay will discover this — the experiment on Day 3 will prove it.
